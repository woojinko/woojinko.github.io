OpenARK

Open-Source Augmented Reality Development Kit

I co-lead and manage the OpenARK project, Berkeley's open-source Augmented Reality SDK. Our team aims to provide companies, researchers, and hobbyists with the ability to quickly create full-scale AR applications and make use of our core functionalities. I personally develop and maintain releases of code for build and install processes behind our core assets such as hand tracking, 3D reconstruction and SLAM. I have created installers and CMake scripts for automating the build and install processes of dependent libraries. Additionally, I am responsible for the full functionality and usability of OpenARK on all Windows systems, documenting issues and debugging guides to be as user-friendly and transparent as possible.

Currently, I am working on ironing out issues and roadblocks on Linux systems to enable Ubuntu OpenARK use for Masters of Engineering VR/AR and CV project teams. I am also currently leading a pipeline project for combining spatial understanding with user experience. The first half involves constructing a 3D scan data pipeline through a deep object detection network for semantic segmentation and an auto-encoder for shape completion. Once that pipeline has been established, the latter half involves creating a module in OpenARK that allows users on their AR interface (mobile or headset) to remove and replace physical objects, attach holograms to specific parts of a scene, and envision potential architectural layouts.
